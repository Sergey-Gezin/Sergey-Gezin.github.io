The mean seats number differs for small and large chains. Let's check the difference for statistical significance:

$H_{0}$ - There is no statistically significant difference between average seats number between large and small chains.
$H_{a}$ - The average seats number is different between small and large chains.

In [22]:
sampleA = obj.query('objects == 1').seats
sampleB = obj.query('objects > 1').seats
print("{0:.3f}".format(st.mannwhitneyu(sampleA, sampleB).pvalue))

0.143

We may reject the null hypothesis since p-value returned by Mann-Whitney test is greater than 0.05. Small chains tend to have more seats than large chains.

Step3. Overall conclusion¶

Restaurants are dominating the market with 75%. On the main streets a succesful small establishment should fit 30-50 people, and medium size establishment should fit 60-150, on peripheral street it is more likely to have up to 30 seats or more than 150. Depending on the restaurant type, there are different seats options: bars and restaurants should have maximum seats capacity, since clients spend there more time. Bars and restaurants are less likely to be a part of a chain. Cafes, fast foods and bakeries are usually a chain establishments.
